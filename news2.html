Quebec reports 146 more COVID-19 cases, four more deaths

Quebec public health officials announced Saturday that 59,458 people have tested positive for COVID-19 in the province since the start of the pandemic and 5,678 people have died due to the novel coronavirus.

McGill students return to campus to protest statue; school admits slave-owning history but says no to statue's removal

A group of students say they won’t give up on removing James McGill’s statue from campus. McGill says it has no plans to do so, but it did, for the first time, add a note to its website acknowledging that its founder was a slave owner.

'So much to learn': The untold stories of slavery in Canada

As monuments to the slavery era in Canada are reconsidered, a small group of Canadian scholars are researching the untold stories of Black and Indigenous people who were enslaved in colonies that became Canada to fill in historical gaps.
